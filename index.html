Valerie Chen

I'm a fifth year Machine Learning PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Ameet Talwalkar. I also spend time at All Hands AI working with Graham Neubig and am a visiting researcher at the NYU Center for Data Science with He He. I previously interned at Microsoft Research in the FATE (Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, and Ethics of AI) group with Q. Vera Liao and Jennifer Wortman Vaughan. My work has been recognized by a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, CMU Presidential Fellowship, and Rising Stars in Data Science.

I study how to build productive human-AI teams by designing (1) new interaction mechanisms between human and AI and (2) scalable, interactive evaluation paradigms.

Previously, I completed my BS in Computer Science at Yale University, where I worked with Zhong Shao and Abhinav Gupta. I have also spent time at IBM Research and the Naval Research Laboratory.
