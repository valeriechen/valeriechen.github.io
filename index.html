Valerie Chen

I'm a second year Machine Learning PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Ameet Talwalkar and my PhD is supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. I completed my BS in Computer Science at Yale University, where I previously worked with Zhong Shao and Abhinav Gupta. I have also spent time at IBM Research and the Naval Research Laboratory.

I am broadly interested in developing new algorithms and theory in the fairness, accountability, and transparency space. I am currently working on automating the evaluation of interpretability methods in an end-task-grounded fashion. I am also collaborating with multiple domain experts to conduct evaluations in practice and understand their unique contexts of using interpretability.

I have also been thinking about how the notion of transparency can be utilized in automated decision-making systems and how to build a mathematically grounded framework to study human-in-the-loop learning.
